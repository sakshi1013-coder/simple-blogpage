Hip hop

Hip hop began in the streets of the Bronx in the 1970s and has grown into a global cultural movement. From DJ Kool Herc’s block parties to Kendrick Lamar’s Pulitzer Prize-winning lyrics, the genre has evolved far beyond just beats and rhymes. Today, hip hop reflects powerful stories of identity, resistance, and community.

Blues

The blues is more than music—it's emotion poured into melody. Emerging from the Deep South in the late 19th century, the blues gave voice to the struggles and resilience of African American communities. With legends like B.B. King and Muddy Waters, the genre shaped rock, jazz, and soul, and continues to inspire artists today with its raw storytelling and unmistakable guitar licks.

K-pop

K-pop isn't just a genre—it's a global phenomenon. Blending catchy melodies, sharp choreography, and stunning visuals, K-pop has redefined what it means to be a pop star. With groups like BTS, BLACKPINK, and EXO leading the charge, K-pop has captured hearts across continents and created passionate fandoms that drive trends, charts, and social change.
